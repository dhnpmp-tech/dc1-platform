# Phase 1 User Interview Guide

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Target Users:** Early-adopter renters (3-5 participants)
**Timeline:** Week 2 (2026-03-31 to 2026-04-02)
**Format:** Zoom video conference

---

## Interview Objectives

Understand real user pain points, model selection criteria, pricing sensitivity, and feature requests from Phase 1 early adopters.

---

## Interview Structure

### Opening (1-2 min)
- Welcome and thank you
- Confirm recording permission (with Zoom recording enabled)
- Overview: "We're learning from your Phase 1 experience to shape the next features"

### Section 1: User Context (2-3 min)
**Goal:** Understand user background and use case

1. "Tell me briefly about your background—what's your expertise?"
2. "What brought you to DCP during Phase 1? What problem are you trying to solve?"
3. "How familiar were you with GPU marketplaces before Phase 1?"

### Section 2: Model Selection & Pricing (4-5 min)
**Goal:** Understand buying decisions and price sensitivity

1. "Walk me through how you chose which model to deploy. What were the deciding factors?"
2. "How important was pricing compared to other factors (latency, availability, support)?"
3. "On a scale 1-10, how would you rate DCP's pricing vs competitors you know about?"
4. "What pricing model would make you use DCP more frequently?"

### Section 3: User Experience (3-4 min)
**Goal:** Identify UX friction points

1. "What was the easiest part of deploying a model on DCP?"
2. "What was the hardest or most confusing part?"
3. "If you could change one thing about the interface, what would it be?"
4. "Did you find the documentation clear? Any gaps?"

### Section 4: Feature Requests (2-3 min)
**Goal:** Understand desired future capabilities

1. "What feature would make DCP more valuable to you?"
2. "Are there any use cases you'd like to run but can't today?"
3. "Would you like batch job support, scheduled inference, or something else?"

### Section 5: Recommendation (1-2 min)
**Goal:** Measure Net Promoter Score (NPS)

1. "How likely are you to recommend DCP to a colleague (1-10 scale)?"
   - **9-10:** Promoter
   - **7-8:** Passive
   - **0-6:** Detractor

2. **If Detractor:** "What would need to change to make you recommend us?"

### Closing (30 sec)
- "Thank you for your time. We'll use this feedback to improve Phase 2."
- Offer incentive if applicable (e.g., $25 credit, free tier upgrade)

---

## Interview Guide Notes

### Recording & Consent
- Inform user about recording at start
- Store recording securely in UX Researcher folder
- Transcribe within 48 hours
- Keep raw transcript + sentiment analysis

### Probing Techniques
- **"Tell me more about..."** when you need deeper insight
- **"Can you walk me through..."** for process-oriented questions
- **"Help me understand..."** to avoid leading questions
- **Listen 70%, talk 30%** — let user elaborate

### Red Flags to Listen For
- Confusion about pricing model
- Difficulty deploying model
- Unmet use case (batch jobs, scheduled inference)
- Negative sentiment about competitor comparison
- Low NPS score with concerns about reliability

### Follow-Up Questions (if needed)
- "You mentioned [X], can you tell me more about that?"
- "Did that affect your decision to use DCP?"
- "How often do you face this problem?"
- "Would you pay for a solution to that problem?"

---

## Interview Outcomes

### Data to Capture
- Recording + transcript
- User sentiment (NPS score + quote)
- 3-5 key insights per interview
- Feature request categorization
- Competitive comparison notes

### Post-Interview Processing
1. Transcribe within 24 hours
2. Extract quotes and insights
3. Categorize features by frequency
4. Compile into "Week 2 Insights Report" (due 2026-04-02)
5. Share high-priority findings with product team

## Candidate Selection Criteria

**Ideal Profile:**
- Deployed at least one model on Phase 1 DCP
- Active during Days 4-6 (2026-03-26 to 2026-03-28)
- Willing to record interview (verbal consent)
- English-speaking (for transcription clarity)
- Represents diverse use cases (inference, fine-tuning, research)
